What Fire Teaches Us About AI

When early humans discovered fire, it wasn’t a tool—it was a force.

It could cook or destroy, comfort or burn. The relationship was one of respect, not control.

AI is Our Fire Now

Not just a tool. A system-shaping, context-altering force.

And like fire, how we use it—or don’t—defines our wisdom more than our access.

Set Boundaries

Let’s set some boundaries—not out of fear, but out of foresight.

What AI Is

  • 🧩 Pattern recognizer: Finds shapes in noise. Simulates well, understands nothing.
  • 📢 Multiplier of intent: Amplifies bias or brilliance.
  • 🦎 Context chameleon: Adapts fast, lacks grounding.

When to Use It

  1. Reveal complex patterns: Protein folding, supply chain risks.
  2. Augment judgment: Co-pilot, not autopilot.
  3. Scale exploration: Test views, assumptions, prototypes.
  4. Handle repetitive work: Invoices, emails, summaries.

When Not to Use It

  1. Accountability is key: No human = no ownership = blame shield.
  2. Relationships matter: Coaching, therapy, teaching.
  3. High-stakes domains: Complex systems need human sense-making.
  4. It’s faster to think: If debugging takes longer than doing—don’t.

A Better Question

Not “What can AI do?”

But “What does it change?”

What AI Changes

If it drafts your reports, what’s left of your point of view?

If it decides for you, what becomes of your judgment?

This Isn’t a Sermon

It’s a reminder. Fire taught us power needs a place.

So we built hearths—not to cage it, but to live with it.

Where will you place yours?
